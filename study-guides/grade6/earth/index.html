Detailed Study Guide | Grade 6 Geography

1. Earth's Rotation

1

24 Hours

Earth spins around its own axis from West to East. One full turn takes roughly 24 hours. We call this period a day. This rotation sets the biological rhythm for almost all life on Earth.

2

Day and Night

Rotation is the direct cause of the day/night cycle. At any moment, the Sun illuminates only half of the Earth (Day), while the other half remains in shadow (Night).

3

Apparent Motion

Because Earth spins toward the East, celestial bodies (Sun, Moon, stars) appear to move from East to West. This is an optical illusion; we are the ones moving, not the sky.

4

Sun Time vs. Standard Time

Did you know that when your watch says 12:00, it might not be real noon?

Sun Time (Local Time): This is nature's time. Noon happens exactly when the Sun is at its highest point in the sky. Because the Earth is curved, this time is slightly different for every single line of longitude.

Standard Time (Zone Time): To make travel easier, huge regions agree to use the same time.

Example: In Poland (Time Zone UTC+1), when the clock says 12:00 PM, the "real" Sun Time in Warsaw is actually 12:24 PM!

5

Time Zones & The Date Line

Earth spins 15° every hour, creating different times across the globe.

Examples: When it is 12:00 PM (Noon) in London, it is already 1:00 PM in Warsaw, but only 7:00 AM in New York.

International Date Line: There is an imaginary line in the Pacific Ocean (180° meridian). If you cross it traveling West, you instantly jump forward to the next day!

2. Earth's Revolution

6

365 Days + 6 Hours

Earth orbits the Sun in an elliptical path. One full lap takes 365 days, 5 hours, and 49 minutes. Every 4 years, we add these hours up (4 × 6h = 24h) to add an extra day (Feb 29), creating a Leap Year.

7

Fixed Axial Tilt

Earth's axis is tilted at an angle of 66°33' relative to its orbit. Crucially, the axis always points the same way in space (toward the North Star, Polaris). This constant tilt is the main cause of seasons.

8

Sun's Height (Altitude) & Shadows

The tilt causes the Sun's height in the sky to change throughout the year:

  • Summer: Sun is high. Rays are concentrated (hot). Shadows are short.
  • Winter: Sun is low. Rays are spread out (cold). Shadows are long.
9

The 4 Key Dates

  • Mar 21 (Spring Equinox): Sun over Equator. Day = Night everywhere.
  • Jun 22 (Summer Solstice): Sun over Tropic of Cancer. Longest day in North.
  • Sep 23 (Autumn Equinox): Sun over Equator. Day and Night duration is equal everywhere.
  • Dec 22 (Winter Solstice): Sun over Tropic of Capricorn. Shortest day in North.
10

Distance Changes

Earth's orbit is not a perfect circle, so our distance from the Sun changes.
